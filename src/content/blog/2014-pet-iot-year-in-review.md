End of 2014. First full year of pet IoT in the house. Time to grade the year and forecast 2015.

(No 2013 forecast to grade against — the series started October 2013 with [Atom and Whistle](/blog/atom-arrives-whistle-activity-monitor-launches/), too late in the year for predictions.)

## What got added in 2014

- **Whistle Activity Monitor v1** (October 2013, full year of use).
- **Whistle Activity Monitor v2** (hardware refresh shipped in October 2014, $129). Marginal improvements: better battery (10 days claimed), better water resistance, better mount. Bought one to compare; using the v1 still because the v2 didn't justify the swap.
- **Joule's microchip** (April 2014, with the kitten herself — implanted at the shelter before adoption).
- **An unsuccessful PetCube purchase** (June 2014). Interactive laser-pointer camera marketed for cats. Joule was unimpressed; Atom thought the laser was the most exciting thing ever, ate the camera (well, the camera survived; the household harmony did not). Returned.

## What worked this year

- **Whistle activity tracking for Atom.** Year-over-year, the data shows a clear shift from puppy bursts (5-15 minute play + 45-90 minute naps) to adult-dog patterns (longer continuous activity, fewer naps). The breed-baseline comparison features were noise (Labs vary widely), but the dog's own historical baseline is genuinely useful.
- **The microchip-as-passive-IoT.** Joule's been to the vet twice; both times the scanner read her chip correctly on the first pass. The system works.

## What didn't

- **The Whistle "social" features.** Whistle pushes "compare your dog to other dogs in your neighborhood" — a leaderboard for activity. Doesn't motivate me at all and probably stresses anxious dog owners.
- **PetCube for cats.** The whole "interactive camera" category needs to figure out that cats don't engage with the same toy more than 3 minutes. Move on.
- **Petnet Smart Feeder.** Backed the Kickstarter in 2014; shipping has slipped from early-2015 to mid-2015. Watching.

## Forecast for 2015

| # | Prediction | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Whistle ships a GPS-enabled model (or acquires Tagg) | 70% |
| 2 | Tagg's parent (Snaptracs/Qualcomm) divests or shutters | 50% |
| 3 | First connected cat-specific product reaches retail (smart cat door with app, or smart litter box with WiFi) | 60% |
| 4 | Petnet ships to backers in 2015 | 75% |
| 5 | A pet camera category emerges (Furbo, Pawbo, PetCube next-gen) | 80% |
| 6 | I install a SureFlap microchip cat door for Joule | 90% |
| 7 | FitBark Gen 2 ships | 70% |
| 8 | A vet integration platform tries to launch ("export your dog's data to your vet"). Won't get adoption. | 50% |

The interesting question isn't whether the dog side grows (it will). It's whether the cat side gets *any* meaningful product this year.

## What I'm buying in 2015

- A SureFlap Microchip Pet Door (manual, not connected) for Joule when she's old enough for outdoor access.
- Whatever Whistle ships next.
- Maybe an early Furbo or Pawbo dog camera if reviews are kind.
- Probably the Petnet feeder when (if) it actually ships.
