## The five WooCommerce launch mistakes the course explicitly fixes

**Skipping the staging site.** New store owner installs WooCommerce on production, plays with checkout settings, breaks the cart 30 minutes before a planned launch. Course remedy: every change goes through a staging site, then gets promoted. Module 2 walks the staging setup in 20 minutes.

**The wrong payment gateway.** Owner picks PayPal Standard because it's the default, then discovers it redirects customers off-site and tanks conversion by 30%. Course remedy: Stripe-first for cards, PayPal as a *secondary* option, with the security checklist in module 4.

**The "no tax zones" mistake.** Owner ships to all 50 states without configuring sales-tax nexus. Six months later, an audit. Course remedy: the per-state tax zone setup, the *who actually owes you tax* table, and the TaxJar/Avalara integration walkthrough.

**The "free shipping on everything" mistake.** Owner toggles free shipping to feel competitive, then discovers margin evaporates. Course remedy: the *shipping cost should be visible somewhere* principle — either bundled into price or shown at cart, but never absorbed silently.

**The plugin-stacking mistake.** Owner installs 14 plugins to add features, every page-load takes 7 seconds, Google ranks them last. Course remedy: the "lean stack" — six plugins that earn their keep, plus the audit pattern for the rest.

## When NOT to use WooCommerce

The course is honest about this: if you're shipping more than ~200 orders a day, or if you have a complex multi-vendor model, *Shopify Plus is probably worth the money.* WooCommerce shines from zero to about 1,000 orders/month. Past that, you're paying in maintenance what you would've paid Shopify in subscription — and getting less.
