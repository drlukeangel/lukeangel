## Common stakeholder-management traps the course teaches you to avoid

**Treating stakeholder management as a one-time activity at kickoff.** Stakeholders change. People move teams. Sponsors get reassigned. The person who approved the project in month one is often not the person you need to convince in month four. The course teaches a *quarterly re-mapping* discipline — short, fast, but recurring.

**Confusing "informed" with "engaged."** Sending a stakeholder a status report doesn't mean they're aligned. It means they got an email. The difference matters when you need their buy-in for a scope change. The course covers the difference and how to design comms for *each* level on the engagement ladder.

**Over-engaging the wrong stakeholders.** Time spent over-communicating with low-power-low-interest stakeholders is time stolen from the ones who actually move the project. The course walks through the *time budget* per quadrant — and how to push back when leadership asks you to "loop in" everyone.

**Mistaking polite agreement for actual support.** Stakeholders in formal meetings often nod along even when they disagree. The real signal comes from what happens between meetings — do they advocate for your project in their own forums? Do they answer your Slack messages? The course teaches the four behavioral tells of *real* support vs polite tolerance.

**Forgetting that stakeholders talk to each other.** Conflicting messages to different stakeholders catch up with you. The course teaches building a single source of truth ("here's what the project is, in 3 paragraphs") that every stakeholder hears the same way.

## The single quickest engagement boost the course teaches

Stop sending stakeholders status *reports*. Start sending stakeholders *one-question prompts* in their preferred channel. "Quick question — does the latest scope draft fit the integration window your team owns?" lands very differently from a 12-page deck. The reply rate jumps from 10% to 70%. The course walks the templates by stakeholder type — exec sponsor, peer team lead, contractor, regulator — in module 7.
