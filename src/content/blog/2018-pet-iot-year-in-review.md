End of 2018.

## Scoring the 2017 forecast

| Prediction | Confidence | Outcome | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fi smart collar ships | 70% | Closed Series A; not shipped yet — 2019 | ✗ (partial; well-positioned) |
| Litter-Robot III Connect ships | 90% | Yes, October — installed in our house | ✓ |
| Petnet first multi-day outage | 65% | Three single-day outages but no multi-day catastrophe yet | ✗ (still hanging on) |
| Consumer microchip-activated feeder | 60% | SureFlap's microchip feeder shipped (UK only — US release delayed) | ✓ (partial; not US yet) |
| Whistle Switch / Whistle 4 with health sensors | 50% | Rumored; not announced | ✗ |
| HomeKit-certified pet camera | 35% | Nothing | ✗ |
| Mars acquires another pet-tech startup | 70% | Mars Petcare acquired AniCura (vet network) — bigger than expected | ✓ |
| Pet-health-data-to-vet platform traction | 35% | Nothing meaningful | ✗ |

3/8 hits + 2 partials = ~45%. **Worst year of forecasting**. Most misses were timing (Fi ships in 2019 instead of 2018, SureFlap feeder US-delayed, Whistle Switch announced 2019, etc.). The "Mars acquires" hit was bigger than expected — AniCura is a 200-clinic vet network, suggesting Mars's strategy is consolidating *both* devices AND vet care.

The forecasting humility lesson: pet IoT is a slow-shipping category. Vendor timelines I expected to be 6-12 months are routinely 12-24 months.

## What got added this year

- **Litter-Robot III Connect** (November). Joule's onboard; great so far.
- **A second PetSafe Smart Feed backup feeder** (now have one for Atom in addition to Joule's). Petnet-resilience strategy.
- **Whistle 2 on Atom decommissioned** (August — battery degraded past usable). Replacement: nothing. Atom went bare-collar for four months. Decision: wait for Fi.

## What worked

- **Litter-Robot III Connect**. Genuine quality-of-life improvement + meaningful health-tracking potential.
- **SureFlap Hub continues reliable**. Joule's outdoor patterns now have 18 months of data.
- **The two-feeder redundancy**. Petnet missed three feeds this year; backup feeder caught them.

## What didn't

- **Petnet's continued slow decline**. Each outage is short but the frequency is rising. The company hasn't communicated about it.
- **The Mars-Whistle relationship** continues to suck. Whistle's API restrictions tightened post-acquisition; community integrations broken. My pet-data export workflow is now mostly Litter-Robot + SureFlap; Whistle's contribution is increasingly limited.
- **Atom's lack of collar tracker for 4 months**. I miss the activity data.

## What I'm buying in 2019

- Fi smart collar when it ships.
- A second Litter-Robot if/when we add another cat (not in 2019 plans, but watching).
- Probably ignoring whatever Whistle ships post-Mars — going to vote with my wallet.

## What's next

The Fi launch is the big event of 2019. The Petnet question — does it survive — is the slow-burn worry. The smart-litter category I expect to grow now that Litter-Robot has proven the concept.

Six years of pet IoT documented. Four pets remaining (one dog, one cat, no additions). The cat-IoT category is finally real. The dog-IoT category is consolidating into Mars's portfolio.
