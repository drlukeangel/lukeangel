## The four MS Project reporting traps the course teaches around

**The "raw Gantt is the report" trap.** PM exports the entire Gantt chart to PDF and emails it. It's 14 pages, illegible to anyone without MS Project context, and useless for decision-making. Course remedy: the *executive Gantt* — collapsed to milestone-only, color-coded by status, on a single landscape page.

**The "every metric on one dashboard" trap.** Custom report has 18 fields, takes 30 seconds to render, and stakeholders skim past it. Course remedy: the *three-card* layout — schedule health, budget health, scope health — each on its own card with a one-line headline.

**The "Excel pivot rebuild every Friday" trap.** PM exports CSV, manually pivots in Excel, formats, emails. Five hours a week. Course remedy: the *Visual Reports* feature exports a live pivot that refreshes automatically — module 3 walks the setup.

**The "no baseline" trap.** Schedule slips, but with no baseline saved nobody can prove or even measure the slip. Course remedy: baseline-at-kickoff discipline, rebaseline only at formal change-control gates, and the variance dashboard in module 5.

## The one MS Project habit that prevents 80% of reporting pain

**Save a baseline at project kickoff. Save a re-baseline at every formal change-control gate.** That's it. Without baselines, every variance report is a fiction. With baselines, the variance reports write themselves and the conversations with leadership become *factual* instead of *opinion*.

## When to retire MS Project entirely

The course is honest about this: if your project lives in continuous iteration with no fixed deadline (most pure-agile software work), MS Project is the wrong shape. *Pick Jira/Linear/Asana and don't apologize for it.* MS Project earns its keep on projects with hard milestones, dependencies between teams, and a finance department that needs predictable variance reporting. The PMs who use it for the wrong shape of project are the ones who eventually rip it out and write a bitter blog post about it. **Use the right tool for the work.**
