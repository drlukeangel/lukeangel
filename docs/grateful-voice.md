# Grateful — how to write for /gratitude/

A daily acknowledgment of one thing worth noting. **Not a blog post about that thing — the thing itself.**

## The rule in one line
**Imply the why. Never explain it.** If you have to explain the joke, it's not funny — same here.

## The shape
- Name the thing. Stop.
- If a second sentence adds something the reader couldn't infer, keep it. If it just spells out what the first sentence already implies, cut it.
- Use Luke's exact phrasing when he hands you raw ideas. Don't paraphrase him into "writing."

## Examples that work (in the collection)
- *Fourteen hours of sunshine.*
- *Trash pickup and the people who do it.*
- *A clear sky and an empty inbox at the same time, briefly.*
- *The water sensor screamed at 2am. It was right. The basement is dry.*
- *Brown v Board of Education was decided today in 1954. For nine people who got it unanimously right when "right" was unpopular.*

## Drafts that failed — and why

| First draft | Cut to | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| *"Six months in and my workout partner is still showing up at 6 AM. Half the gratitude is the workout. The other half is the showing up — most people stop."* | *"Six months in and my workout partner is still showing up at 6 AM."* | The first sentence already implies everything the second two spell out. |
| *"Trash truck came at 7. The people riding the back of it keep a city from collapsing into itself, and I never see them. Worth seeing them once in a while."* | *"Trash pickup and the people who do it."* | Trying to add weight that's already there. |
| *"Engineer at the all-hands: 'is AI going to replace me?' Speaker: 'not if you don't let it.' Best answer I've heard all year. The lever is yours."* | *"Engineer at the all-hands: 'is AI going to replace me?' Speaker: 'not if you don't let it.'"* | The exchange IS the post. Adding "the lever is yours" is the explanation. |
| An 80-word Manchester Baby essay listing Williams tubes, the 32-word memory, the seven instructions, the 52-minute runtime. | *"Manchester Baby ran the world's first stored-program today in 1948. Williams & Kilburn at the U. of Manchester. Every computer since runs on what worked that morning."* | If you're naming people and specifying technical detail, you're writing a Wikipedia entry, not a gratitude post. |

## Topic + level
Pick `topic` from the enum in `src/content/config.ts` — `team`, `home`, `readers`, `world`, `tech`, `everyday`, `craft`, `trailblazers`. `level` is `small` / `medium` / `large` — about *significance*, not length. A single sentence can be `medium` if the thing it names is load-bearing.

## When in doubt
Cut the closing sentence.
